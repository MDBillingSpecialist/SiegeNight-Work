[i]Every night could be your last.[/i]

Siege Night turns your base into a battleground. Every few nights, the dead march on your position from a single direction -- wave after wave, growing stronger as the night goes on. Build your walls. Stock your ammo. Survive until dawn.

Built for fortress defense. Solo or multiplayer.

[list]
[*] [b]Wave Cycles[/b] -- WAVE (dense assault), TRICKLE (stragglers), and BREAK (patch up and reinforce). 3 to 7 waves per night, escalating as midnight approaches.
[*] [b]Directional Attacks[/b] -- every siege picks one of 8 compass directions and all zombies come from that angle for the entire night. Different directions each siege force real decisions about where your walls go.
[*] [b]Siege Scaling[/b] -- zombie count scales with sieges survived and players online. Early game: 3 waves, ~75-150 zombies. Late game: 6-7 waves, up to 1,500.
[*] [b]Per-Group Independent Sieges[/b] -- in MP, groups in different areas each get their own siege with separate waves, direction, kill tracking, and clear condition.
[*] [b]Safehouse Anchor Mode[/b] -- zombies target your claimed safehouse instead of your player position. They attack your base even if you leave.
[*] [b]Dawn Survival[/b] -- make it to sunrise and your character announces the kill count. The siege panel tracks everything.
[*] [b]30+ Sandbox Options[/b] -- tune siege frequency, zombie count, scaling, spawn distance, anchor mode, special zombie rates, mini-horde thresholds, and more. Spread across 3 sandbox pages.
[*] [b]Kill Tracking[/b] -- a Siege Night tab in your character info (press H) shows wave/phase, kills this siege, lifetime totals, and full siege history for the last 20 nights.
[/list]

Between siege nights, your activity generates [b]heat[/b]. Generators (+15), gunfire (+10), driving (+8), heavy construction (+5). Heat builds around your location and decays slowly when you go quiet.

Push your luck too far and you'll hear: [i]"Something's attracting them from the south..."[/i]

Mini-hordes come from the direction of your heat source. An attractor system pulls them toward you. Kill enough and the attractor shuts off -- the rest scatter. Cooldown between triggers and a daily cap so you won't get chain-hit.

[h1]Recommended MP Baseline ("Normal")[/h1]

If SiegeNight feels like it's dumping hundreds of zombies on you early (ex: day 3 Rosewood), these are a good starting point for multiplayer.

Note: SiegeNight is still being balanced. The defaults are intentionally flexible, not a tuned "Normal" difficulty yet.

Quick scaling tip (MP): if you have more players, raise caps; if you have fewer, lower them.
- 1-2 players: BaseZombieCount 25-40, MaxActiveZombies 120-160
- 3-5 players: (baseline below)
- 6+ players: BaseZombieCount 60-80, MaxActiveZombies 250-350

[b]SiegeNight[/b]
[list]
[*] FirstSiegeDay: [b]5[/b]
[*] FrequencyDays: [b]5[/b]
[*] BaseZombieCount: [b]50[/b]
[*] MaxActiveZombies (per group): [b]200[/b]
[*] MaxZombies (per siege): [b]800[/b]
[/list]

[b]Mini-Hordes[/b]
[list]
[*] MiniHorde_NoiseThreshold: [b]100[/b]
[*] MiniHorde_CooldownMinutes: [b]60[/b]
[*] MiniHorde_MaxPerDay: [b]2[/b]
[*] MiniHorde_MaxZombies: [b]35[/b]
[*] MiniHorde_MinZombies: [b]8[/b]
[/list]

These settings keep the "fortress defense" vibe but give you time to establish a base, and they prevent back-to-back mini-hordes from spiraling the early game.

[h1]Commands[/h1]

[list]
[*] [b]!siege start[/b] -- Start a siege immediately (admin only in MP)
[*] [b]!siege stop[/b] -- End the current siege (admin only in MP)
[*] [b]!siege status[/b] -- Show current siege state, kills, and progress
[*] [b]!siege next[/b] -- Show when the next siege is scheduled
[*] [b]!siege vote[/b] -- Start a vote to trigger a siege (any player)
[*] [b]!siege yes[/b] -- Vote yes on an active vote
[*] [b]!siege optout / optin[/b] -- Opt out/in of sieges
[/list]

Vote system needs majority of online players. 60 second window. SP uses Numpad debug keys (Numpad 0 to enable, then 2/7/9/6 for controls).
